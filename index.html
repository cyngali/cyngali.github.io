Bubble tea aka Boba

What is bubble tea?

Basic definition of Boba

Bubbletea(also known as Boba) is a tasty and unique, southeast asian drink invented in the 1980s.
The base of the drink is tea, combined with fruit and/or milk flavors. What makes it so unique is that you can personalize your drink by adding toppings along with the flavoring. This makes for a refreshing drink and tasty snack you can drink AND eat!

One of the most popular toppings used in Bubbletea is Boba. Which is why oftentimes Bubbletea is simply refered to as "Boba".
Boba is made out of the taro root, which is similar to a sweet potatoe or yam. From the taro is made small bite size balls that are typically sweetened with a brown sugar syrup. Adding boba to a drink can sweeten it and make for a chewy treat.

Where does Bubble tea originate from?

The famous drink known as Boba originated in Taiwan.
It's popularity then spread throughout Japan and Hong Kong. From there, it spread by trade throughout the Chinatowns across North America.

Today, Bubble tea/Boba can be found in almost all cities in North America and even throuhgout the world. Fun Fact! Bubble tea is consumed almost as frequently as coffee, soda, and smoothies. In order to spread the bubble tea culture even more, the Boba company Kung Fu Tea founded the National Bubble Tea Day on April 30th 2018. Make sure to mark your calendars so you can celebrate this year. Some Boba shops might even host special events or have discounted prices on this day! Make sure to check them out.
